# Exp 012 Summary: OTA5T DC Characterization

## Scope

This experiment added and ran DC-focused OTA testbenches to evaluate:

- Differential I-V transfer with fixed DC output load.
- Unity-gain (non-inverting follower) rail-to-rail DC sweep.
- Differential I-V saturation scaling versus bias current.

## Testbenches

- `tb_dc_iv_transfer.asdl`
  - Differential sweep (`Vinp` swept, `Vinn` fixed at `VCM`).
  - Output clamped by a fixed DC source (`Vout = 1.65 V`) to measure output current.
- `tb_dc_unity_rr_sweep.asdl`
  - Unity follower (`out -> inn`) with rail-to-rail input sweep.
- `tb_dc_iv_transfer_ibias_050u.asdl`
- `tb_dc_iv_transfer_ibias_200u.asdl`
  - Same I-V setup as nominal, with bias-current corners.

## Key Findings

### 1) Differential I-V (nominal `IBIAS=100uA`)

- I-V curve is monotonic and shows clear large-signal saturation.
- Measured current range: about `-208.94 uA` to `+195.95 uA`.
- Small offset observed:
  - `Iout(Vdiff=0) ~ -7.97 uA`
  - zero crossing at `Vdiff ~ -7.16 mV`
- Around `Vdiff=0`, extracted transconductance magnitude is about `1.11 mA/V`.

### 2) Unity-gain rail-to-rail DC sweep

- Follower tracks well over much of the range but is not ideal rail-to-rail.
- Endpoint behavior:
  - `Vout(Vin=0) ~ 0 V`
  - `Vout(Vin=3.3V) ~ 3.212 V`
- Maximum tracking error is large in low-input region:
  - `max |Vout - Vin| ~ 471 mV` (near `Vin ~ 0.51 V`)

### 3) Saturation scaling vs bias current

Plateau current scales near-linearly with bias current:

- `IBIAS=50uA`  -> plateau magnitude about `~100 uA`
- `IBIAS=100uA` -> plateau magnitude about `~200 uA`
- `IBIAS=200uA` -> plateau magnitude about `~400 uA`

This matches expected OTA behavior for this topology:

- Tail current is mirror-related to the bias branch and sets the large-signal steering limit.
- At large differential input, nearly all tail current is steered to one side, so output current saturates near that limit.

## Interpretation

- The new DC benches successfully expose both intended behaviors:
  - differential transconductor saturation,
  - unity-follower tracking limits.
- The measured I-V scaling strongly supports a tail-current-limited large-signal model.
- Remaining asymmetry/offset and follower low-end error are realistic non-ideal behaviors and good targets for follow-on OP/corner analysis.
